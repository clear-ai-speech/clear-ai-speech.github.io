ClearAI: AI-Driven Speech Enhancement for Patients with Hypophonia

Yuanda Wang1,    Qiben Yan1,   Thea Knowles1,   Daryn Cushnie-Sparrow2,   Scott Adams2

1Michigan State University, East Lansing, USA    2Western University, London, Canada

Abstract

Hypophonia—characterized by unusually low volume and a breathy voice quality—is a prevalent speech impairment in Parkinson’s disease that severely limits intelligibility and effective communication. The weak acoustic signal not only challenges human listeners but also degrades the performance of voice-controlled systems, especially under noisy conditions.

Conventional enhancement methods typically amplify overall loudness or suppress background noise, yet they fall short of restoring the clarity and naturalness required for hypophonic speech. To bridge this gap, we introduce ClearAI, an AI-driven framework purpose-built to improve speech quality for individuals with hypophonia. ClearAI leverages voice-conversion techniques to construct a parallel corpus of healthy and hypophonic utterances. A predictive model, trained on this augmented dataset, estimates an optimal speech style that simultaneously strengthens vocal intensity and refines prosodic patterns while preserving speaker identity.
